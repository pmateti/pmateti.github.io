  3. (40 points) You are given a large (in the zillions) bag B of pairs (c, m) where c is a lower case letter and m is an integer.   We would like to sum up the numbers m corresponding to each of the letters c.  That is, we would like to reduce B to a set S of letter-number pairs (d, n), where d is a letter that has appeared in a pair of B, and n is the sum of the corresponding numbers m that were paired with d.  No two pairs of S have the same letter d.  Every letter that appeared in a pair of B also appears in a pair of S. Two solutions, one in CSP and another in C-Linda are to be developed.  It is not required that both use the same "idea."  Present your solutions with full explanations. 
    (a) (10 points) As usual, (i) we wish to maximize concurrency, (ii) we prefer a symmetric solution, (iii) we rate correctness much higher than efficiency, and cannot tolerate (iv) deadlocks or (v) livelocks.  Defend why for each of these five.
    (a) (15 points) For C-Linda, assume that the tuple space already contains the <"c", c, m>, and the size of this bag <"bsz", bsz>. You lose 5 points for each use of inp or rdp.  Use an appropriately chosen number of processes.
    (b) (15 points) For CSP, the bag is generated by process B whose structure is B :: *[ ... --> P!(c, m) ].  Develop the process P and an appropriately chosen other processes.

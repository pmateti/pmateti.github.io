This is a closed book, closed notes exam, except for the few items listed in the last class. Do not give or take help during the exam.
  1. (12*5 points) In the  following, it is possible to write no more than, say, ten, sentences each, and yet receive full score.  Explain/ discuss/ dispute/ compute/ prove.
    1. There are examples of safety properties that are also liveness properties.
    2. Detection of termination is difficult only in peer-to-peer distributed computing not in client-server computing.
    3. Construct the ssend(B, msg) and srecv(B, msg) primitives of synchronous message passing from asend(B, msg) and arecv(B, msg) primitives of asynchronous message passing.
    4. Token-passing algorithms were discussed under the heading of asynchronous message passing because token-passing does not make sense under synchronous message passing.
    5. The happened before relation, as discussed, requires a logical clock for every process.  But  a typical machine (node) in a distributed system runs several processes. It is sufficient to maintain a logical clock per node that is shared by all processes running on that node.
    6. Reconstruct the binary "tree" serialized below. List each node as a triplet of (info integer, left-link and right-link), as usual.  The first digit in the sequence is the offset of the root node.
      marshaled sequence: 2 1 4 2 6 3 4 9 4 0 2 9 9
      offsets in hex:     1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 A B C D
    7. Does the precondition and statement {x  ≥ 4 } < x := x - 4 > interfere with the triple
      {x is odd} < x := x + 5 > {x is even}.
    8. Using the technique of weakened assertions, prove that {x = 0} S {x = 9} is a theorem, where S is var x := 0; co <x := x+2> || <x := x+3> || <x := x+4> oc
    9. Explain the Receive Axiom: {P} receive ch(vars) {Q}.
    10. Discuss why it is difficult to implement a semaphore in the truly distributed context.
    11. Can any solution to the distributed mutual exclusion problem be thought of as a solution to the problem of implementing semaphores in a distributed system, and vice versa.
    12. Explain, with an example, the map/reduce idea behind Hadoop.
  2. (10+15+15 points) Given a large (in the zillions) bag B of integers, find the third largest number in B.  Assume that B has at least three distinct integers. Two solutions, one in CSP and another in C-Linda are to be developed.  It is not required that both use the same "idea."  Present your solutions with full explanations.  (a) (10 points) As usual, (i) we wish to maximize concurrency, (ii) we prefer a symmetric solution, (iii) we rate correctness much higher than efficiency, and cannot tolerate (iv) deadlocks or (v) livelocks.  Defend why for each of these five.  (b)  For C-Linda, assume that the tuple space already contains the bag of integers <"B", n>, the size of this bag <"BSZ", bsz>, and <"p", p> the number of worker processes.. You lose 5 points for each use of inp or rdp.  (c)  For CSP, the bag is generated by process B whose structure is B :: *[ ... --> P!n ].  Develop the process P and an appropriately chosen other processes.

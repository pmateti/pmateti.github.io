  4. (30 points) Given a bag B of integers, and the size b of it. It is known that each element in the bag appears exactly d times. We wish to delete the duplicates resulting in a set of only b/d distinct elements that occurred in the bag. It is guaranteed that d > 0, b > 0. Unfortunately, the value d is unavailable and needs to be computed by examining the bag B. (i) Design (15 points, with full explanation) and (ii) implement (15 points) a solution in C-Linda. Assume that the tuple space already contains <"B", xi> for all xi in B, and the size of the bag <"sizeB", b> . You lose 5 points for each use of inp or rdp. As usual, we wish to maximize concurrency, we prefer a symmetric solution, and correctness rates much much higher than efficiency.  If you present (i) and (ii) in a combined way, you will receive (partial) credit only for the weaker of the two.

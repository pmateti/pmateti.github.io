Mid Term Exam 1
April 25, 2001   100 points max   75 minutes
Prabhaker Mateti, Spring Quarter 2001
This is a closed book/notes/... exam.
   
  1. (35; 5 points each) Describe, in a few lines, the following.
    1. Routing table.
    2. Spoofing.
    3. Smurfing.
    4. Public-key encryption scheme.
    5. Backdoors and rootkits.
    6. Script kiddie.
    7. Proper configuration versus Fortification versus Hardening.
  2. (5+15 points) (a) In the context of passwords, explain salt, and its purpose.  (b) Assume that passwords are limited to using the 26 lowercase letters, and the 10 digits.  Assume that all passwords are between 5 to 10 characters in length.  Assume a password cracker with an encryption rate of 6.4 million encryptions per second.  How long will it take to test exhaustively all possible passwords?  Show all your work.  Calculations need not be exact; order of magnitude calculations will do.
  3. (5+15 points)  Consider the following five significant events that occur in the booting of a Unix machine from power on to login prompt. The events may or may not occur in the order shown. E1. Root volume is mounted; E2. Process init is created;  E3 inetd daemon is started; E4. OS Boot loader invokes the kernel;  E5.  Getty processes are started.  (a)  Arrange the events in chronological order, and explain the events further.  (b) In each of these, describe how security may have been breeched.
  4. (15+10 points) Suppose that an attacker has acquired privileges to read/write/execute any file on a Unix system.   Suppose his/her goal is to obtain one hundred userid-password combinations, and replace what ever changes he/she may have made with their originals so that this activity has a greater chance of unnoticed.  (a) Describe what files are changed where and how. (b) What steps could a system administrator have taken so that this activity was noticed?

Proper Configuration

These are notes to myself and Chris Roberts as we prepare to produce the lecture videos. Expected video length of this "chapter": 60 minutes. Each line item below is worth between 30 sec to 2 minutes.

Abstract: We distinguish hardening a system from proper configuration and fortification. Hardening, in our definition, refers to areas of security tightening during the design and construction of systems rather than after their deployment. Carefully adjusting various parameters after an out-of-the-box install is configuration. Installing or uninstalling various packages using the OS native tools and mechanisms, such as apt-get in Debian/ Ubuntu, is fortification. For further elaboration, read the Proper Configuration article/video.
